<p><p>The PTCA balloon catheter market includes various types designed for treating coronary artery disease. Normal balloon catheters expand vessels to restore blood flow. Drug-eluting balloon catheters release medication to prevent restenosis. Cutting balloon catheters feature tiny blades to modify plaque during inflation, enhancing vessel dilation. Scoring balloon catheters have raised areas along the surface, allowing for more precise plaque disruption. Each type serves specific clinical needs, contributing to patient outcomes in coronary interventions.</p></p>

<p><p>The PTCA balloon catheters market is categorized into semi-compliant and non-compliant segments, each serving distinct applications in coronary angioplasty. Semi-compliant balloons expand under low pressure, adapting to vascular contours, ideal for gradual dilatation and achieving optimal lumen size. In contrast, non-compliant balloons maintain their shape and diameter at high pressures, providing precise and focused dilation on stenotic lesions. This differentiation enhances procedural effectiveness and patient outcomes, driving demand within interventional cardiology settings for optimal coronary artery treatment.</p></p>
